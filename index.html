Why LJI for Me

Most loyalty platforms treat personalization as a features race — more tiers, more rewards, better UI. GRAVTY® is asking a different question: what does loyalty look like when you can model behaviour in real time across 400 million members?

That's not a marginal improvement. That's a structural shift in what enterprise loyalty can actually do for a brand. I've been building toward this problem — on smaller scale, with less data — for most of my career. The problems I find genuinely hard are the ones LJI is working on.

Hyderabad is home. The scale is real. The timing is right. This is where I want to build next.

About & Philosophy

I'm a Product Manager with 10 years of building products that sit at the intersection of real human problems and serious business scale — from a 1M+ MAU consumer social app to FDA-regulated life sciences platforms trusted by global teams.

I've shipped features used by hundreds of thousands of people. I've also sat in rooms where a wrong product call had regulatory consequences. That range — consumer intuition on one side, enterprise rigour on the other — is what I bring to every problem I walk into.

These days I'm equally obsessed with what AI makes possible. On the side, I'm building B2C micro-SaaS products and consumer apps — some utilitarian, some just fun. Building is how I think and learn. Nothing sharpens product instincts faster than owning the whole stack yourself.

01
Problems before solutions, always
I've seen too many teams fall in love with a feature before they've understood the pain. My first instinct is to slow down and make sure we're solving the right thing — then move fast.
02
Strategy has to survive contact with engineering
A beautiful roadmap that falls apart in sprint planning isn't strategy — it's a wish list. I stay close to the build to close the gap between vision and execution.
03
Data tells you what. Users tell you why.
Metrics show you a drop-off. They won't tell you why the CTA is in the wrong language or the flow assumes a mental model your user doesn't have. I use both, and I don't confuse one for the other.
04
The best PMs make teams feel the mission
Roadmap alignment and sprint rituals are all in service of one thing: a team that ships with conviction. That's the environment I try to create.
